# PIA06605: Epimetheus Alone | [ ![Epimetheus Alone](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/browse/PIA06605.jpg) ](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpegMod/PIA06605_modest.jpg) | | Target Name: | **[Epimetheus](http://solarsystem.nasa.gov/planets/profile.cfm?Object= Epimetheus)** ---|--- Mission: | ** [Cassini](http://saturn.jpl.nasa.gov) ** Spacecraft: | ** [Cassini Orbiter](http://saturn.jpl.nasa.gov) ** Instrument: | ** Imaging Science Subsystem - Narrow Angle ** Product Size: | **450 samples x 493 lines** Produced By: | ** [CICLOPS/Space Science Institute](http://ciclops.org) ** Full-Res TIFF: | ** [PIA06605.tif](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA06605.tif) (70.42 kB) ** Full-Res JPEG: | ** [PIA06605.jpg](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA06605.jpg) (7.594 kB) ** Medium-Res JPEG: | ** [PIA06605_modest.jpg](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/ jpegMod/PIA06605_modest.jpg) (7.59 kB)** **Original Caption Released with Image: ** One of Saturn's strange co-orbital moons, Epimetheus, was captured by Cassini in this view. Irregularly shaped Epimetheus occasionally swaps orbits with nearby Janus (see [PIA06603](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA06603)), and both moons play a role in maintaining the outer edge of Saturn's bright A ring. Epimetheus is 116 kilometers (72 miles) across. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Jan. 22, 2005, at a distance of approximately 2.5 million kilometers (1.6 million miles) from Epimetheus and at a Sun-Epimetheus-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 90 degrees. Resolution in the image is 15 kilometers (9 miles) per pixel. The image has been contrast-enhanced to aid visibility.
