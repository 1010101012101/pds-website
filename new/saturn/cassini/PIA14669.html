Saturn is now almost exactly halfway between its equinox (August 2009) and southern winter solstice (in May 2017). At equinox, the rings' shadows appeared as a thin line at Saturn's equator. See [PIA11667](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA11667). This view is centered on an area at 22 degrees south latitude on Saturn. The image was taken with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera on May 6, 2013 using a spectral filter sensitive to wavelengths of near-infrared light centered at 752 nanometers. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 813,000 miles (1.3 million kilometers) from Saturn. Image scale is 48 miles (78 kilometers) per pixel.
