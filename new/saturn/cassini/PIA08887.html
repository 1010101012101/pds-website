The moon's basic, elongated shape is easy to detect here. (See [PIA08233](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA08233) for a different perspective on Atlas.) Above Atlas (32 kilometers, or 20 miles across) in the image, a bright clump in the F ring also heads toward the darkness. This view looks toward the unlit side of the rings from about 30 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Jan. 19, 2007 at a distance of approximately 1.2 million kilometers (700,000 miles) from Atlas.
