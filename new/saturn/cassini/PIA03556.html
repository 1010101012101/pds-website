The ring is the bluest in the center, where the gravitational clumps are the largest. The thickest black band in the ring is the Enke Gap, and the thin black band further to the right is the Keeler Gap. The right image is a computer simulation about 150 meters (490 feet) across illustrating a clumpy region of particles in the A ring. The particles are moving counterclockwise, from bottom to top.
