The planet wears the shadow of its rings as a dark belt. Just above that belt is the shadow of 181-kilometer (113-mile) wide Janus. This view was acquired from 38 degrees above the Saturn's equator. The image was taken with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera on Jan. 13, 2008 using a spectral filter sensitive to wavelengths of infrared light centered at 752 nanometers. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1.2 million kilometers (746,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 68 kilometers (42 miles) per pixel.
