The images show an object-interior to and detached from the bright core of the F ring that appears to be breaking up into discrete clumps. Cassini scientists have been monitoring clumps in the F ring for more than two years now, trying to understand whether these represent small permanent moonlets or transient aggregates of material. (See [PIA07716](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA07716).) This view looks toward the unlit side of the rings from about 35 degrees above the ringplane. The images were taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow- angle camera on Dec. 23, 2006 at a distance of approximately 2 million kilometers (1.2 million miles) from Saturn.
