--- layout: base layout_style: default title: PIA09810: Warp Zone --- # PIA09810: Warp Zone [ ![ Warp Zone](browse/PIA09810.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA09810_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA09810_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA09810.jpg) (22.65 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA09810.tif) (717.2 kB) **Caption:** A small moon appears from behind giant Saturn, accompanied by a warped view of the rings. Prometheus (102 kilometers, or 63 miles across) is seen here between the A and F rings. Close to the planet, the image of the rings is slightly distorted by Saturn's upper atmosphere. This view is similar to [PIA09807](PIA09807.html), which features Pandora. This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 9 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible red light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Nov. 30, 2007. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 1.6 million kilometers (1 million miles) from Prometheus. Image scale is 10 kilometers (6 miles) per pixel.
