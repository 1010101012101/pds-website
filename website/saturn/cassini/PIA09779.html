The rings are squashed into a narrow band from this viewing angle, foreshortening all of their radial features. Mimas (397 kilometers, or 247 miles across) travels from left to right here, led by its large crater Herschel. The image was taken in visible green light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow- angle camera on Oct. 26, 2007. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1.1 million kilometers (661,000 miles) from Mimas. Image scale is 6 kilometers (4 miles) per pixel.
