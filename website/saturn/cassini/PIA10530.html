Whether it was Prometheus or Pandora that created the kink is not obvious in this instance. While not as impressive as the gores which Prometheus induces in the ring, the gravitational influence of this little moon is still important in shaping the F ring. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Oct. 15, 2008 at a distance of approximately 1.026 million kilometers (637,000 miles) from Saturn and at a Sun-Saturn-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 38 degrees. Image scale is 6 kilometers (4 miles) per pixel. The Cassini-Huygens mission is a cooperative project of NASA, the European Space Agency and the Italian Space Agency.
