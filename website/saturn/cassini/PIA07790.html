--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA07790: Four Propellers [ ![ Four Propellers](browse/PIA07790.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA07790_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA07790_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA07790.jpg) (160.7 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA07790.tif) (703.2 kB) **Caption:** These figures show four propeller-shaped structures discovered by the Cassini spacecraft in close-up images of Saturn's A ring. The propellers are about 5 kilometers (3 miles) long from tip to tip, and the radial offset (the "leading" dash is slightly closer to Saturn) is about 300 meters (1,000 feet). See [PIA07791](PIA07791.html) and [PIA07792](PIA07792.html) for additional images and information about these features. The figures were cropped from two original Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera images and magnified for visibility. The images were then re-projected so that orbital motion is to the left and Saturn is up. The unseen moonlets lie in the center of each structure. The figures were cropped from two original Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera images, taken during Saturn orbit insertion on July 1, 2004, and magnified for visibility.
