A hint of the moon's active south polar region can be seen as a just slightly dark area at bottom. This view was obtained from about 1 degree above the ringplane. Enceladus is 505 kilometers (314 miles) across. The image was taken in polarized green light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Oct. 27, 2007. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 2.1 million kilometers (1.3 million miles) from Enceladus. Image scale is 13 kilometers (8 miles) per pixel.
