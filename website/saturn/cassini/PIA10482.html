Seen here at center is the Cassini Division, flanked at top and bottom by the outer B-ring edge and the inner A-ring edge, respectively. The gravitational influence of the moon Mimas is responsible for the Cassini Division. See [PIA08389](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA08389) for a labeled map of Saturn's rings. The view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 33 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Aug. 2, 2008. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1 million kilometers (639,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 6 kilometers (4 miles) per pixel.
