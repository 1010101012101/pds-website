--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA08311: Toying with Gravity" --- # PIA08311: Toying with Gravity [ ![ Toying with Gravity](browse/PIA08311.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA08311_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA08311_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08311.jpg) (14.35 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08311.tif) (215.8 kB) **Caption:** This view, like [PIA08303](PIA08303.html), shows Prometheus with a streamer it has created in the inner edge of the F ring. Prometheus comes close to the inner edge of the ring once per orbit, perturbing the ring particles there. In 2009, the moon's orbit is expected to carry it repeatedly into the F ring core, an event that ring scientists are eagerly awaiting. Prometheus is 102 kilometers (63 miles) across. This view looks toward the unlit side of the rings from about 40 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Oct. 16, 2006 at a distance of approximately 1.8 million kilometers (1.1 million miles) from Prometheus and at a Sun-Prometheus-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 150 degrees. Scale in the original image was 11 kilometers (7 miles) per pixel. The image has been magnified by a factor of two.
