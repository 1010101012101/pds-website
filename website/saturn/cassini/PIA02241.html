--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA02241: Saturn's rings [ ![Saturn's rings](browse/PIA02241.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA02241_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA02241_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA02241.jpg) (86.52 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA02241.tif) (599.2 kB) **Caption:** When seen from the unlit side, the rings of Saturn present a much different appearance from that familiar to telescopic observers. Relatively opaque areas like the B Ring turn black, while lightly populated zones, such as the C Ring and the Cassini Division, prove to excellent diffuse transmitters of sunlight. The A Ring, with intermediate opacity, is at an intermediate level of brightness. **Image Note:** Voyages to Saturn NASA SP-451NASA Scientific and Technical Information Branch 1982caption (page 171) PICNO 1023S1-001 Source: [NASA's Planetary Photojournal: Image No. PIA02241](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA02241)
