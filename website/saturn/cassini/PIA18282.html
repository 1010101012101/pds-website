--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA18282: Dot Against the Dark" --- # PIA18282: Dot Against the Dark [ ![ Dot Against the Dark](browse/PIA18282.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA18282_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA18282_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA18282.jpg) (76.48 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA18282.tif) (1.042 MB) **Caption:** As if trying to get our attention, Mimas is positioned against the shadow of Saturn's rings, bright on dark. As we near summer in Saturn's northern hemisphere, the rings cast ever larger shadows on the planet. With a reflectivity of about 96 percent, Mimas (246 miles, or 396 kilometers across) appears bright against the less-reflective Saturn. This view looks toward the sunlit side of the rings from about 10 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera on July 13, 2014 using a spectral filter which preferentially admits wavelengths of near-infrared light centered at 752 nanometers. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 1.1 million miles (1.8 million kilometers) from Saturn and approximately 1 million miles (1.6 million kilometers) from Mimas. Image scale is 67 miles (108 kilometers) per pixel at Saturn and 60 miles (97 kilometers) per pixel at Mimas.
