F-ring shepherding moon Pandora (81 kilometers, or 50 miles across), along with the inner shepherd moon Prometheus (see [PIA09887](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA09887)), helps to keep the narrow lanes of the F ring in check. This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 15 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on April 5, 2008. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1.3 million kilometers (814,000 miles) from Pandora. Image scale is 8 kilometers (5 miles) per pixel.
