--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA09780: Saturnian Citizens" --- # PIA09780: Saturnian Citizens [ ![ Saturnian Citizens](browse/PIA09780.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA09780_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA09780_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA09780.jpg) (36.17 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA09780.tif) (935.5 kB) **Caption:** Cassini spies two icy denizens of the Saturn System as they hurtle past. The view captures Mimas (397 kilometers, or 247 miles across) at bottom, with Epimetheus (116 kilometers, or 72 miles across) about 48,000 kilometers (30,000 miles) farther beyond. This scene looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 3 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible green light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Nov. 6, 2007. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 2.8 million kilometers (1.8 million miles) from Mimas. Image scale is approximately 17 kilometers (11 miles) per pixel on both moons.
