Mimas (397 kilometers, or 247 miles across, at bottom) is easily identified by its prominent crater, Herschel. Rhea (1,528 kilometers, or 949 miles across) sits beyond the rings, appearing almost to rest upon them. This view was obtained from a perspective nearly edge-on with the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on July 6, 2007 at a distance of approximately 2.6 million kilometers (1.6 million miles) from Mimas and 3.2 million kilometers (2 million miles) from Rhea. Image scale is 15 kilometers (9 miles) per pixel on Mimas and 19 kilometers (12 miles) per pixel on Rhea.
