--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA08227: Shade from Epimetheus" --- # PIA08227: Shade from Epimetheus [ ![ Shade from Epimetheus](browse/PIA08227.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA08227_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA08227_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08227.jpg) (49.91 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08227.tif) (869.1 kB) **Caption:** The shadow of Epimetheus, one of Saturn's co-orbital moons, races across the planet's restless cloud tops. Epimetheus (116 kilometers, or 72 miles across) cruises along beyond the orbits of the narrow F ring and its shepherd moons. Farther south on Saturn, the swirls and eddies are obscured by the shadow of the outer A ring and its two prominent, moon-containing gaps, Encke (bottom) and Keeler (dim, at the A ring edge). The image was taken with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera using a spectral filter sensitive to wavelengths of infrared light centered at 728 nanometers. The view was obtained on June 30, 2006 at a distance of approximately 335,000 kilometers (208,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 16 kilometers (10 miles) per pixel.
