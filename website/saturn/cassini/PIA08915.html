--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA08915: Astral Pearl" --- # PIA08915: Astral Pearl [ ![ Astral Pearl](browse/PIA08915.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA08915_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA08915_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08915.jpg) (2.362 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08915.tif) (95.79 kB) **Caption:** The high northern latitudes on Enceladus show little detail from Cassini's distant vantage point, nearly 50 degrees above the moon's equator. The plume of icy material that jets from the south pole of Enceladus (505 kilometers, or 314 miles across) is not visible in this viewing geometry. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Feb. 26, 2007 at a distance of approximately 1.7 million kilometers (1.1 million miles) from Enceladus and at a Sun-Enceladus-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 95 degrees. Scale in the original image was 10 kilometers (6 miles) per pixel. The image was contrast enhanced and magnified by a factor of two.
