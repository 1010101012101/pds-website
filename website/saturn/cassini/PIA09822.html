--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA09822: Pan in View [ ![ Pan in View](browse/PIA09822.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA09822_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA09822_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA09822.jpg) (48.93 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA09822.tif) (1.025 MB) **Caption:** A small ring-embedded moon coasts into view from behind shadow-draped Saturn. The rings' image is distorted near Saturn by the planet's upper atmosphere, to the right of Pan (26 kilometers, or 16 miles across). This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 8 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera Dec. 22, 2007. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 1.8 million kilometers (1.1 million miles) from Pan. Image scale is 11 kilometers (7 miles) per pixel on Pan.
