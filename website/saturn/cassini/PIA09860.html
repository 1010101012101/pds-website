--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA09860: Rings Aglow [ ![ Rings Aglow](browse/PIA09860.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA09860_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA09860_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA09860.jpg) (68.85 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA09860.tif) (2.938 MB) **Caption:** Saturn's softly glowing rings shine in scattered sunlight. The B ring presents a remarkable difference in brightness between the near and far arms (bottom and top of the image, respectively). The strong variation in brightness could be due to the presence of wake-like features in the B ring. See [PIA08389](PIA08389.html) for a labeled Cassini map of the rings. This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 5 degrees above the ringplane. Images taken using red, green and blue spectral filters were combined to create this natural color view. The images were acquired at a distance of approximately 574,000 kilometers (357,000 miles) from Saturn. At the center of the image, the Sun-ring-spacecraft, or phase, angle is 114 degrees, and the image scale is 34 kilometers (21 miles) per pixel in the radial, or outward from Saturn, direction.
