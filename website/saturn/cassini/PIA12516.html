Pandora (81 kilometers, or 50 miles across) orbits outside the F ring and, in this image, is farther from Cassini than the rings are. This view looks toward the northern, sunlit side of the rings from just above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Nov. 8, 2009. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 2.4 million kilometers (1.5 million miles) from Pandora. Image scale is 14 kilometers (9 miles) per pixel.
