This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 15 degrees above the ringplane. Tethys is 1,062 kilometers, or 660 miles, across. The image was taken in visible red light with the Cassini spacecraft wide- angle camera on May 13, 2008. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1.3 million kilometers (836,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 81 kilometers (50 miles) per pixel.
