Brightness variations are clearly visible in the Encke ringlet. A density wave can be seen exterior to (to the left of) the Encke gap, and the region exterior to (to the left of) the Keeler gap is considerably brighter than the rest of the ring. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Oct. 23, 2008 at a distance of approximately 721,000 kilometers (448,000 miles) from Saturn and at a Sun-Saturn-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 58 degrees. Image scale is 4 kilometers (2 miles) per pixel. The Cassini-Huygens mission is a cooperative project of NASA, the European Space Agency and the Italian Space Agency.
