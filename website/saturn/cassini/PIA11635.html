--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA11635: Slicing the Arc" --- # PIA11635: Slicing the Arc [ ![ Slicing the Arc](browse/PIA11635.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA11635_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA11635_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA11635.jpg) (73.99 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA11635.tif) (1.029 MB) **Caption:** The bright arc within Saturn's G ring is shown truncated by the shadow of the planet at the bottom of this image. Although it can't be seen here, the tiny moonlet Aegaeon (formerly known as S/2008 S 1) orbits within the bright arc. See [PIA11148](PIA11148.html) to learn more. This view looks toward the northern, sunlit side of the rings from about 4 degrees above the ringplane. Many background stars are visible elongated by the motion of the spacecraft during the image's exposure. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Oct. 9, 2009. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 2.1 million kilometers (1.3 million miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 12 kilometers (7 miles) per pixel.
