--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA10233: Saturn's Ring Rhythm #2 [ ![ Saturn's Ring Rhythm #2](browse/PIA10233.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA10233_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA10233_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10233.jpg) (270.3 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10233.tif) (7.323 MB) **Caption:** For an Earth observer on May 3, 2005, the Cassini spacecraft appeared to pass behind the rings, then Saturn, then the rings again (the red line). The discovered harmonic structure was found on both the way in and out, but only in locations where particles are densely packed together, such as the B ring and the innermost part of the A ring. The May 3 radio experiment and several others to follow in 2005 showed that the regular spacing of the harmonic structure vary from 100 to 250 meters (320 to 820 feet), depending on the location in the rings.
