The view shows a portion the rings from the outer B ring, at upper right, to the F ring at bottom. See [PIA08389](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA08389) for a labeled map of Saturn's rings. The perspective is toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 15 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Dec. 12, 2007. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 2.2 million kilometers (1.4 million miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 28 kilometers (17 miles) per pixel in the radial, or outward from Saturn, direction; and 13 kilometers (8 miles) per pixel in the longitudinal, or around Saturn, direction.
