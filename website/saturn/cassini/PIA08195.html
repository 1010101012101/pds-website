--- layout: base layout_style: default title: PIA08195: Dark Side of Saturn's Moon --- # PIA08195: Dark Side of Saturn's Moon [ ![ Dark Side of Saturn's Moon](browse/PIA08195.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA08195_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA08195_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08195.jpg) (22.95 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08195.tif) (577.5 kB) **Caption:** Cassini stares toward the night side of Saturn, seen here on the right, as the active icy moon Enceladus glides past. The moon's now famous icy plumes spew out of the south polar region (see [PIA07758](PIA07758.html)), providing a fresh supply of material for Saturn's E ring. Enceladus is 505 kilometers (314 miles) across. Saturn's shadow stretches over the rings above the crescent moon. The image was acquired in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on May 4, 2006 at a distance of approximately 2.1 million kilometers (1.3 million miles) from Enceladus and 2.3 million kilometers (1.5 million miles) from Saturn. The image was taken at a Sun-Enceladus-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 159 degrees. Image scale is 13 kilometers (8 miles) per pixel on Enceladus.
