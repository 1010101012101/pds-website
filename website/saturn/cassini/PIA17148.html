--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA17148: Splitting the F Ring" --- # PIA17148: Splitting the F Ring [ ![ Splitting the F Ring](browse/PIA17148.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA17148_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA17148_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA17148.jpg) (46.74 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA17148.tif) (1.033 MB) **Caption:** Saturn's F ring often appears to do things other rings don't. In this Cassini spacecraft image, a strand of ring appears to separate from the core of the ring as if pulled apart by mysterious forces. Some ring scientists believe that this feature may be due to repeated collisions between the F ring and a single small object. Eight stars are also visible in this image. This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 49 degrees below the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Oct. 19, 2013. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1.2 million miles (1.9 million kilometers) from Saturn and at a Sun-Saturn-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 120 degrees. Image scale is 6.8 miles (11 kilometers) per pixel. The Cassini-Huygens mission is a cooperative project of NASA, the European Space Agency and the Italian Space Agency.
