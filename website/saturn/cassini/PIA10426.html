--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA10426: Beyond the Limb [ ![ Beyond the Limb](browse/PIA10426.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA10426_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA10426_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10426.jpg) (42.86 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10426.tif) (1.021 MB) **Caption:** Saturn's C ring emerges from behind the planet's hazy limb. This view looks toward the sunlit side of the rings from about 15 degrees below the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on June 17, 2008. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 937,000 kilometers (582,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 5 kilometers (3 miles) per pixel. The Cassini-Huygens mission is a cooperative project of NASA, the European Space Agency and the Italian Space Agency. The Cassini orbiter and its two onboard cameras were designed, developed and assembled at JPL. The imaging operations center is based at the Space Science Institute in Boulder, Colo.
