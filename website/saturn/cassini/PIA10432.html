--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA10432: Obscuring Epimetheus [ ![ Obscuring Epimetheus](browse/PIA10432.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA10432_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA10432_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10432.jpg) (41.29 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10432.tif) (593.7 kB) **Caption:** Saturn's narrow F ring partly obscures the small moon Epimetheus. Interior to the F ring is the bright A ring. Epimetheus (113 kilometers, or 70 miles across at its widest point) is on the side of the rings opposite from Cassini. This view looks toward the sunlit side of the rings from about 2 degrees below the ringplane.The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on June 11, 2008. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 1.2 million kilometers (766,000 miles) from Epimetheus. Image scale is 7 kilometers (5 miles) per pixel.
