Waves like these allow scientists to locate small moons in gaps and measure their masses. For more on Daphnis (5 miles, or 8 kilometers across) and the Keeler gap, see [PIA11655](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA11655) and [PIA06238](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA06238). This view looks toward the sunlit side of the rings from about 13 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Aug. 14, 2012. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 483,000 miles (778,000 kilometers) from Saturn and at a Sun-Saturn-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 4 degrees. Image scale is 3 miles (4 kilometers) per pixel. The F ring has been brightened by a factor of 1.5 relative to the main rings to enhance visibility.
