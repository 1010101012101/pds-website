--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA05413: Saturn's Atmosphere and Rings [ ![Saturn's Atmosphere and Rings](browse/PIA05413.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA05413_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA05413_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA05413.jpg) (37.52 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA05413.tif) (507.3 kB) **Caption:** In this image, dark regions represent areas where Cassini is seeing into deeper levels in Saturn's atmosphere. The dark regions are relatively free of high clouds and the light at these particular near-infrared wavelengths (centered at 727 nanometers) penetrates into the gaseous cloud-free atmosphere and is absorbed by methane. The image was taken with the Cassini spacecraft narrow angle camera on May 15, 2004, from a distance of 24.7 million kilometers (15.4 million miles) from Saturn. The image scale is 147 kilometers (91 miles) per pixel. Contrast in the image was enhanced to aid visibility.
