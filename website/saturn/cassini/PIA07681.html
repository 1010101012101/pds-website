--- layout: base layout_style: default title: PIA07681: Prometheus with Distant Dione --- # PIA07681: Prometheus with Distant Dione [ ![ Prometheus with Distant Dione](browse/PIA07681.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA07681_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA07681_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA07681.jpg) (17.56 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA07681.tif) (1.04 MB) **Caption:** The ring moon Prometheus continues its work shaping the delicate F ring as Dione looks on. It is easy to see how Prometheus has an irregular, oblong shape, while Dione is quite round. The rings are partly cut off by Saturn's shadow at right. Prometheus is 102 kilometers (63 miles) wide; Dione is 1,123 kilometers (700 miles) wide. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Dec. 20, 2005, at a distance of approximately 2.5 million kilometers (1.6 million miles) from Dione and 2.2 million kilometers (1.4 million miles) from Prometheus. The image scale is 15 kilometers (9 miles) per pixel on Dione and 13 kilometers (8 miles) per pixel on Prometheus.
