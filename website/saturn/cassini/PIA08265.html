Where they meet the limb, the rings appear to bend slightly owing to upper-atmospheric refraction. Crenulations --irregularly wavy or serrated features -- in the planet's clouds denote the locations of turbulent belt/zone boundaries. The image was taken using a spectral filter sensitive to wavelengths of infrared light centered at 728 nanometers. The view was obtained with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera on Aug. 16, 2006 at a distance of approximately 256,000 kilometers (159,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 12 kilometers (7 miles) per pixel. The Cassini-Huygens mission is a cooperative project of NASA, the European Space Agency and the Italian Space Agency.
