--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA09912: Splinter of Light [ ![ Splinter of Light](browse/PIA09912.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA09912_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA09912_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA09912.jpg) (34.23 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA09912.tif) (1.042 MB) **Caption:** A sliver of "ringshine" pierces the darkness of Saturn's night side. This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 58 degrees above the ringplane. The ring shadows fall into darkness beyond the terminator in the north. South of the equator, a dim glow brightens the darkened globe. This light, called ringshine, comes from sunlight reflected off the sunward side of the expansive rings (the opposite face of the ringplane from this perspective). The effect is pronounced in the eclipse view [PIA08329](PIA08329.html). The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera on April 19, 2008. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 851,000 kilometers (529,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 48 kilometers (30 miles) per pixel.
