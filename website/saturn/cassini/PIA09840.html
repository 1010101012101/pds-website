At the top of the image, between the spacecraft and the rings, is Epimetheus. The darker region extending from center right is within the C ring. Surrounding it are the B ring, Cassini Division and A ring. This view looks toward the sunlit side of the rings from about 2 degrees below the ringplane. Epimetheus is 116 kilometers (72 miles) across. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Jan. 17, 2008. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 1.1 million kilometers (707,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is about 6 kilometers (4 miles) per pixel.
