--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA09805: Saturn's Outer C Ring" --- # PIA09805: Saturn's Outer C Ring [ ![ Saturn's Outer C Ring](browse/PIA09805.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA09805_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA09805_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA09805.jpg) (90.57 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA09805.tif) (1.042 MB) **Caption:** This view takes in the outer third of Saturn's C ring—from the Maxwell Gap, at center left, to the C-ring edge at lower right. For reference, see the labeled mosaic of the rings presented in [PIA08389](PIA08389.html). This view looks toward the sunlit side of the rings from about 4 degrees below the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Nov. 17, 2007. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 465,000 kilometers (289,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale at the center of this view is 3 kilometers (2 miles) per pixel in the radial, or outward from Saturn, direction and 42 kilometers per pixel in the longitudinal, or around Saturn, direction.
