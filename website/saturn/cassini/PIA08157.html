--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA08157: Crescent Moon with Rings [ ![ Crescent Moon with Rings](browse/PIA08157.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA08157_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA08157_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08157.jpg) (7.279 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08157.tif) (358.9 kB) **Caption:** This poetic scene shows the giant, smog-enshrouded moon Titan behind Saturn's nearly edge-on rings. Much smaller Epimetheus (116 kilometers, or 72 miles across) is just visible to the left of Titan (5,150 kilometers, or 3,200 miles across). The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on March 9, 2006, at a distance of approximately 4.1 million kilometers (2.5 million miles) from Titan. The image scale is 25 kilometers (16 miles) per pixel on Titan. The brightness of Epimetheus was enhanced for visibility.
