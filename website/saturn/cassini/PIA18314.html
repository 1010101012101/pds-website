--- layout: base layout_style: default title: PIA18314: Serene Saturn --- # PIA18314: Serene Saturn [ ![ Serene Saturn](browse/PIA18314.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA18314_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA18314_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA18314.jpg) (40.69 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA18314.tif) (1.05 MB) **Caption:** From a distance Saturn seems to exude an aura of serenity and peace. In spite of this appearance, Saturn is an active and dynamic world. Its atmosphere is a fast-moving and turbulent place with wind speeds in excess of 1,100 miles per hour (1,800 km per hour) in places. The lack of a solid surface to create drag means that there are fewer features to slow down the wind than on a planet like Earth. Mimas, to the upper-right of Saturn, has been brightened by a factor of 2 for visibility. In this view, Cassini was at a subspacecraft latitude of 19 degrees North. The image was taken with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera on Feb. 4, 2015 using a spectral filter centered at 752 nanometers, in the near-infrared portion of the spectrum. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1.6 million miles (2.5 million kilometers) from Saturn. Image scale is 96 miles (150 kilometers) per pixel.
