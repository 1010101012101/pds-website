--- layout: base layout_style: default --- # PIA09775: Enceladus in Hiding [ ![ Enceladus in Hiding](browse/PIA09775.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA09775_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA09775_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA09775.jpg) (9.834 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA09775.tif) (467.9 kB) **Caption:** From a low angle above Saturn's rings, the Cassini spacecraft's view of an icy moon is partly obscured. The view looks toward Enceladus (505 kilometers, or 314 miles across) across the unilluminated side of the rings from less than a degree above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Oct. 26, 2007. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1.6 million kilometers (1 million miles) from Enceladus. Image scale is 10 kilometers (6 miles) per pixel.
