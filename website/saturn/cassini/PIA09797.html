--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA09797: Mimas Emerges" --- # PIA09797: Mimas Emerges [ ![ Mimas Emerges](browse/PIA09797.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA09797_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA09797_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA09797.jpg) (27.5 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA09797.tif) (800.2 kB) **Caption:** The Cassini spacecraft watches a thin, bright sliver emerge from the hazy limb of Saturn. In one minute, the sliver ballooned into the full disk of Mimas (397 kilometers, or 247 miles across), which coasted silently into the black sky. This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from less than a degree above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Oct. 26, 2007. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1.5 million kilometers (900,000 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is about 9 kilometers (6 miles) per pixel. The Cassini-Huygens mission is a cooperative project of NASA, the European Space Agency and the Italian Space Agency.
