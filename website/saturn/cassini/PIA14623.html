See [PIA12734](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA12734) and [PIA12705](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA12705) to see other dramatic views depicting and explaining the interplay of sunlight between Saturn and its rings. Janus (111 miles, or 179 kilometers across) appears as a small white dot in the bottom left of the view, beyond the rings. Epimetheus (70 miles, or 113 kilometers across) appears in the top left. This view looks toward the southern, unilluminated side of the rings from about 20 degrees below the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera on June 24, 2012. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 1.2 million miles (2 million kilometers) from Saturn and at a Sun-Saturn- spacecraft, or phase, angle of 103 degrees. Image scale is 71 miles (115 kilometers) per pixel. The Cassini-Huygens mission is a cooperative project of NASA, the European Space Agency and the Italian Space Agency.
