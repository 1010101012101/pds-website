--- layout: base layout_style: default title: PIA10523: Ring Shapers --- # PIA10523: Ring Shapers [ ![ Ring Shapers](browse/PIA10523.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA10523_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA10523_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10523.jpg) (27.67 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10523.tif) (1.042 MB) **Caption:** Two moons that have profound impacts on the rings, Mimas and Prometheus, are seen here with the F ring. Mimas (396 kilometers, or 246 miles across), the larger and much more distant of the moons, creates the Cassini division between the A and B rings. Prometheus (86 kilometers, or 53 miles across), although much smaller than Mimas, is half of a duo responsible for maintaining the narrow F ring.
