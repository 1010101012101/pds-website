This "opposition surge" travels across the rings as the spacecraft watches (see [PIA08267](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA08267)). See [PIA08247](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA08247) for a detailed explanation of the opposition effect. This view looks toward the sunlit side of the rings from about 9 degrees below the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera on June 12, 2007 using a spectral filter sensitive to wavelengths of infrared light centered at 853 nanometers. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 524,374 kilometers (325,830 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 31 kilometers (19 miles) per pixel.
