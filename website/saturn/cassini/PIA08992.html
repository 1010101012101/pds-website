--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA08992: Surging Across the Rings" --- # PIA08992: Surging Across the Rings [ ![ Surging Across the Rings](browse/PIA08992.jpg)](jpegMod/PIA08992_modest.jpg) [Medium-Res JPEG](jpegMod/PIA08992_modest.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08992.jpg) (52.48 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08992.tif) (1.042 MB) **Caption:** A surge in brightness appears on the rings directly opposite the Sun from the Cassini spacecraft. This "opposition surge" travels across the rings as the spacecraft watches (see [PIA08267](PIA08267.html)). See [PIA08247](PIA08247.html) for a detailed explanation of the opposition effect. This view looks toward the sunlit side of the rings from about 9 degrees below the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft wide-angle camera on June 12, 2007 using a spectral filter sensitive to wavelengths of infrared light centered at 853 nanometers. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 524,374 kilometers (325,830 miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 31 kilometers (19 miles) per pixel.
