The equatorial region is generally brighter than the rest of the planet in Cassini spacecraft views, but the contrast is often striking in monochrome views like this, taken in the infrared part of the spectrum at wavelengths sensitive to methane absorption in the planet's atmosphere. (Compare, for example, [PIA08392](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA08392) and [PIA07669](http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/catalog/PIA07669).) This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 24 degrees above the ringplane.
