--- layout: base layout_style: default title: PIA01953: The Outer Edge of A Ring --- # The Outer Edge of A Ring ![PIA01953: The Outer Edge of A Ring](PIA01953-med.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA01953.jpg) (41.4 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA01953.tif) (132.8 kB) **Caption:** The outer edge of Saturn's A ring is detailed in this image obtained by Voyager 2 on Aug. 26, just half an hour before closest approach, at a range of about 51,000 kilometers (31,700 miles). Resolution in this wide-angle view is about 4 km. (2.5 miles). The many sharp linear features parallel to the ring edge are most probably locations where ring particles are in [resonance](/glossary.html#resonance) with one of the several small nearby satellites of about 100-km. radius discovered last fall by Voyager 1. Such regions are likely characterized by more intense particle collisions and a greater density of small "chips." The entire outer band of the A ring has different scattering properties and therefore different particle characteristics than the main body of the rings to the upper left.
