--- layout: base layout_style: default title: "PIA01531: Saturn's B and C Rings" --- # Saturn's B and C Rings ![PIA01531: Saturn's B and C Rings](PIA01531-med.jpg) [Full-Res JPEG](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA01531.jpg) (863.2 kB) [Full-Res TIFF](//photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA01531.tif) (15.92 MB) **Caption:** This view focusing on Saturn's C ring (and to a lesser extent, the B ring at top and left) was compiled from three separate images taken through ultraviolet, clear and green filters. On Aug. 23, when it acquired these frames, Voyager 2 was 2.7 million kilometers (1.7 million miles) from the planet. More than 60 bright and dark ringlets are evident here; the small, bland squares are caused by the removal of [reseau](/glossary.html#reseau) (reference) marks during processing. In general, C ring material is very bland and gray, the color of dirty ice. Color differences between this ring and the B ring indicate differing surface compositions for the material composing these complex structures.
