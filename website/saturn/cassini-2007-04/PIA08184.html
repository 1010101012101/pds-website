The edge-on rings cast dramatic shadows onto the northern hemisphere. Janus is 181 kilometers (113 miles) across. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on April 21, 2006, at a distance of approximately 2.9 million kilometers (1.8 million miles) from Saturn. The image scale is 17 kilometers (11 miles) per pixel on Janus. The Cassini-Huygens mission is a cooperative project of NASA, the European Space Agency and the Italian Space Agency.
