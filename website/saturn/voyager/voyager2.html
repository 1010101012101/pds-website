--- layout: base --- # Voyager 2 Images of Saturn's Ring System (August 1981) [ ![](/saturn/voyager/plate_05-tn.gif) The Cassini Division and the A Ring](/saturn/voyager/plate_05.gif) (Color GIF, 239 kbytes). Saturn's outer A Ring in false color, constructed from Voyager 2 green, violet, and ultraviolet frames. The Cassini Division (lower right) is distinctly less red than the A Ring. Also visible, as a sequence of dark features in the outermost A Ring, is a series of orbital resonances with Saturn's inner moons. The 325 km wide Encke Division (upper left center) contains the tiny moon Pan. However, the cause of the narrower Keeler Gap (closer to the outer edge of the A Ring) and the gaps in the Cassini Division is not known. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/plate_06-tn.gif) The B and C Rings in False Color](/saturn/voyager/plate_06.gif) (Color GIF, 306 kbytes). The outer part of Saturn's C Ring (blue) and the inner part of the B Ring (yellow) are shown here in false color. The color difference blurs over the boundary between the rings. The Maxwell Gap and its eccentric ringlet, which appears yellow due to image mis-alignment, are in the left center of this frame. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/saturn53-tn.gif) Chemistry of the Rings](/saturn/voyager/saturn53.gif) (Color GIF, 400 kbytes). This highly enhanced false color view of Saturn's rings was assembled from clear, orange and ultraviolet images. Possible variations in chemical composition from one part of Saturn's ring system to another are visible as subtle color variations. In addition to the previously known blue color of the C Ring and the Cassini Division, the picture shows additional color differences (red-green) within and between the A and B Rings. The distance to Saturn was 8.9 million kilometers (5.5 million miles). [ ![](/saturn/voyager/c4364334-tn.gif) Spokes in the B Ring](/saturn/voyager/c4364334.gif) (B&W; GIF, 288 kbytes). Voyager 2 image [FDS](/glossary.html#fds) 43643.34 shows a distinct pattern of "spokes" in the B Ring, which is the broad, bright, middle ring of Saturn's three main rings. The spokes are made of dust that has been raised off of the ring particles, but the reason the spokes appear is unknown. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/saturn20-tn.gif) The Birth of a Spoke](/saturn/voyager/saturn20.gif) (B&W; GIF, 91 kbytes). Contrast enhancement reveals numerous spokes in the B Ring. In a sequence of four Voyager 2 images (shown here as inset panels), a small spoke is seen to appear in between several more prominent spokes. The arrow in the fourth inset image points to the new spoke. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/saturn40a-tn.gif) Spokes in Forward-Scattered Light](/saturn/voyager/saturn40a.gif) (B&W; GIF, 67 kbytes). After passing Saturn, Voyager 2 viewed the B Ring in forward-scattered light. The spokes, seen previously as dark streaks, are now seen as bright streaks from this [phase angle](/glossary.html#phase_angle). This indicates that the spokes are caused by tiny dust grains, comparable in size to the wavelength of light. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/c4390447-tn.gif) Structure in the B Ring](/saturn/voyager/c4390447.gif) (B&W; GIF, 295 kbytes). The B Ring is made up of narrow ringlets down to the limit of the image resolution. Some darker spokes are also visible. The Cassini Division is visible at upper right, and the A Ring is outside it; the C Ring is visible at lower right. This Voyager 2 image [FDS](/glossary.html#fds) 43904.47. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/c4393531-tn.gif) The B and C Rings](/saturn/voyager/c4393531.gif) (B&W; GIF, 296 kbytes). A closeup of the transition from the faint inner C Ring to the much brighter B Ring. Both rings show fine structure at many scales. This is Voyager 2 image [FDS](/glossary.html#fds) 43935.31. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/saturn34-tn.gif) Closeup of the B Ring](/saturn/voyager/saturn34.gif) (B&W; GIF, 146 kbytes). This is one of Voyager 2's closest looks at the B Ring, from August 25, 1981 at a distance of 743,000 km. The contrast has been enhanced to show the fine structure. The portion of the ring shown here is 6000 km wide and features as narrow as 10 km are visible. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/c4398946-tn.gif) Closeup of the Cassini Division](/saturn/voyager/c4398946.gif) (B&W; GIF, 281 kbytes). The Cassini Division, separating Saturn's A and B Rings, is maintained by the 2:1 [resonance](/glossary.html#resonance) with Mimas. It was once thought to be empty, but this closeup view, Voyager 2 image [FDS](/glossary.html#fds) 43989.46, shows that it really contains several broad belts of material separated by empty "rifts". [ ![](/saturn/voyager/pan-tn.gif) The Discovery of Pan](/saturn/voyager/pan.gif) (Color GIF, 55 kbytes). The 10-km moon Pan [shepherds](/glossary.html#shepherding_satellite) open the 325 km wide Encke Gap in the outer part of Saturn's A Ring. This is the Voyager image in which is was discovered, [FDS](/glossary.html#fds) 43918.04. The region inside the green box is enlarged and enhanced as an inset at upper right to show Pan more clearly. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/saturn92-tn.gif) F Ring and Shepherds](/saturn/voyager/saturn92.gif) (B&W; GIF, 82 kbytes). Two tiny moons, Pandora and Prometheus, are seen here [shepherding](/glossary.html#shepherd_satellite) the F Ring. Prometheus, the inner shepherd, orbits faster and had just overtaken Pandora before this image was taken. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/saturn81-tn.gif) F Ring and Prometheus](/saturn/voyager/saturn81.gif) (B&W; GIF, 78 kbytes). This image shows Prometheus next to the F Ring. Prometheus is one of the [shepherd](/glossary.html#shepherd_satellite) moons associated with the F Ring. This image was taken on August 25, 1981 from a distance of 365,000 km. Note that the moon is distinctly elongated. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/c4400649-tn.gif) Crossing the Ring Plane](/saturn/voyager/c4400649.gif) (B&W; GIF, 141 kbytes). Image [FDS](/glossary.html#fds) 44006.49 shows the F Ring [ansa](/glossary.html#ansa) just before Voyager 2 passed through the ring plane on August 26, 1981. The F Ring seems to be made up of a number of separate strands. In addition, the whole region inside the F Ring is not black, indicating that it is filled with fine dust. [ ![](/saturn/voyager /saturn35a-tn.gif) The Foreshortened Rings](/saturn/voyager/saturn35a.gif) (B&W; GIF, 72 kbytes). The last image taken by Voyager 2 just before it crossed Saturn's ring plane on August 26, 1981. The entire ring system is shown, but it is very nearly edge-on and highly foreshortened. The F Ring shows up prominently in the foreground. [ ![](/saturn/voyager/saturn19-tn.gif) Dark Side of the Rings](/saturn/voyager/saturn19.gif) (Color GIF, 28 kbytes). This color-enhanced Voyager 2 image shows the unlit side of the rings. Since the broad B Ring is very [optically thick](/glossary.html#optical_depth), very little light gets through so it appears very dark here. The A and C Rings let more light through so they appear brighter.
