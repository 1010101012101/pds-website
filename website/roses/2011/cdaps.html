    Data Sources

    The Cassini Data Analysis and Participating Scientists Program (CDAPS) is described in Appendix C.10 and Amendment No. 4 of the ROSES 2011 NRA. The full document can be accessed under Solicitations at the NSPIRES web site.

    Amendment 4 to the NRA, released in early April 2011, adds a Cassini Participating Scientist Program to the original Cassini Data Analysis program (CDAP). It changes the name to the Cassini Data Analysis and Participating Scientists (CDAPS) Program, and it delays the due dates for both notices of intent (NOIs) and proposals. The due date for CDAPS NOIs has been changed to Friday, June 10, 2011. The due date for CDAPS proposals has been changed to Friday, July 8, 2011.

    The specific data availability restrictions given for the 2011 CDAPS are:

      In order to be considered under the data analysis portion of the call, proposing PIs must use data that are in the public domain 90 days before the CDAPS proposals due date, and they must make clear to the peer reviewers that the data are publicly available. This applies to the first year of proposed work only. It is understood that the second or third year of work will address data that have come into the archive during the first year of the investigation. Proposals that do not comply with this rule will be declared noncompliant and will be declined without evaluation. Whether from the PDS or another source, if the data to be analyzed is not certified or otherwise has issues that might represent an obstacle to analysis, the proposers must clearly demonstrate that such potential difficulties can be overcome.

    Finding Cassini Data within the PDS

      OPUS, our Outer Planets Unified Search tool, allows users to search for and obtain subsets of data based on a wide range of constraints. At this time the the tool's support for Cassini data is limited to CIRS, ISS and VIMS and does include the display of products for all three instruments. We plan to continue extending the scope of the tool to address all of the data held at the Rings Node.

    The PDS NAIF Node and observation geometry.

      SPICE data files contain spacecraft and solar system geometry data necessary to interpret scientific observations from space-based instruments. The SPICE system also includes a large suite of software, mostly in the form of subroutines, that users incorporate in their own application programs to read SPICE files and to compute derived observation geometry, such as altitude, latitude/longitude, and lighting angles. SPICE data and software may be obtained from the NAIF web site.
