The Planetary Mission Data Analysis Program (PMDAP) is described in Appendix C.11, ROSES-13 Amendment 16.. The full document can be accessed under Solicitations at the NSPIRES web site.

There are two distinct aspects to the PMDAP AO - Data Analysis and Data Restoration. The criteria for the eligibilty of data are significantly different for the two aspects.

Data Analysis

    Data Sources

      The broad data availability restrictions given for the 2013 PMDAP for data analysis are:

      "The PMDAP supports scientific investigations that use only data available in the Planetary Data System (PDS) (//pds.nasa.gov/) or other publicly accessible archive(s), such as planetary data in the NASA astrophysics data archives. The data must be archived and publicly available 30 days prior to the submission deadline for PMDAP proposals. Spacecraft data that have not been placed in such archives may not be proposed for use in PMDAP investigations."

      "Data products accessible in the PDS must be designated “certified” (ready for scientific use) in order to be eligible for the PMDAP (see Appendix C.1). Data products accessible in archives other than the PDS that are still in liens resolution are not considered archived and their analysis will not be supported by the PMDAP."

      "The PMDAP is not intended to overlap other active data analysis programs. Thus the PMDAP does not support the analysis of:

      • Lunar data
      • Mars data obtained by missions to Mars
      • Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune system phenomena
      • Data from Cassini
      • Data from the New Horizons, Galileo, Voyager, Pioneer, and Ulysses missions"

    While there are some exceptions to these restrictions (e.g., Galileo asteroid flyby data), all of the data at the Ring-Moon Systems Node falls under one or more of the above exclusions.

Data restoration

    PMDAP affords a unique opportunity for ring scientists to propose for the restoration of publicly available data sets. Individuals or teams interested in pursuing this opportunity should contact us early for assistance in identifying and obtaining eligible data, or determining the PDS compliance requirements for the completed deliverables.

    Additional information regarding preparing data for the PDS is available through the Archiving tab.

    • SPICE data and software may be obtained from the NAIF web site.SPICE data files contain spacecraft and solar system geometry data necessary to interpret scientific observations from space-based instruments. The SPICE system also includes a large suite of software, mostly in the form of subroutines, that users incorporate in their own application programs to read SPICE files and to compute derived observation geometry, such as altitude, latitude/longitude, and lighting angles.
