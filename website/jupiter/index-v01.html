--- layout: base layout_style: default --- [ ![\[PIA01621\]](PIA01621_thumb.jpg) ](./galileo/PIA01621.html) # Jupiter's Ring System Jupiter's ring was discovered by Voyager 1 in a single image that was targeted specifically to search for a faint ring system. Subsequently, Voyager 2 was reprogrammed to take a more complete set of images. The ring is now known to be composed of three major components. The _main ring_ is about 7000 km wide and has an abrupt outer boundary 128,940 km from the center of the planet. The main ring encompasses the orbits of two small moons, Adrastea and Metis, which may act as the source for the dust that makes up most of the ring. At its inner edge the main ring merges gradually into the _halo_. The halo is a broad, faint torus of material about 10,000 km thick and extending halfway from the main ring down to the planet's cloudtops.
