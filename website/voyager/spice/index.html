For more information about SPICE visit the home page of PDS's [Navigation and Ancillary Information Facility](http://pds-naif.jpl.nasa.gov) (NAIF). Note that the primary source of SPICE data is NAIF's [anonymous FTP site](ftp://naif.jpl.nasa.gov/pub/naif/). See, in particular: * [/pub/naif/VOYAGER](ftp://naif.jpl.nasa.gov/pub/naif/VOYAGER/): Voyager-related files. * [/pub/naif/generic_kernels](ftp://naif.jpl.nasa.gov/pub/naif/generic_kernels/): Generic kernels, e.g., the latest planetary ephemerides, constants and leap seconds. * [/pub/naif/toolkit](ftp://naif.jpl.nasa.gov/pub/naif/toolkit/): The SPICE software toolkit, ready-made for most popular platforms. * [/pub/naif/toolkit_docs](ftp://naif.jpl.nasa.gov/pub/naif/toolkit_docs/): Users' guides and tutorials. ## The SPICE Tabs ![\[SPACER\]](../css/spacer.gif) The tabs associated with this page describe the SPICE data files and related information---how to obtain it and how to use it. * [Ephemerides](spk.html) \- SP kernels describing the trajectory of the spacecraft and the motion of the planet and satellites during each planetary encounter. * [Pointing](ck.html) \- C kernels and other pointing information for Voyager's instruments. * [Other](other.html) \- Other related kernels helpful in the analysis of Voyager data. * * * [Voyager](/voyager/) | [Rings Node Home](/)
