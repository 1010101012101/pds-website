# Ophir Chasma * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA22xxx/PIA22602_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA22602.jpg) (1368 x 2648) (250.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA22602.tif) (1368 x 2648) (2.4 MB)

Caption:


Context image

Today's VIS image shows part of Ophir Chasma. Ophir Chasma is part of Valles Marineris, the largest canyon system on Mars. At the top of the image is a portion of a large landslide deposit which originate at the northern wall of the canyon. The bright toned part at the bottom of the image is the northern extent of Beatis Mensa, a large layered deposit within the canyon.
