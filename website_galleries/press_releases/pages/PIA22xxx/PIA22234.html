# The InSight Team at JPL * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA22xxx/PIA22234_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA22234.jpg) (4000 x 2527) (1.8 MB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA22234.tif) (4000 x 2527) (29.1 MB)

Caption:

The InSight Team at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, JPL, in June 2015.

The InSight team is comprised of scientists and engineers from multiple disciplines and is a unique collaboration between countries and organizations around the world. The science team includes co-investigators from the U.S., France, Germany, Austria, Belgium, Canada, Japan, Switzerland and the United Kingdom.

Background Info:

JPL, a division of Caltech in Pasadena, California, manages the InSight Project for NASA's Science Mission Directorate, Washington. Lockheed Martin Space, Denver, built the spacecraft. InSight is part of NASA's Discovery Program, which is managed by NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, Alabama.

For more information about the mission, go to https://mars.nasa.gov/insight .
