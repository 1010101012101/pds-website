# Kasei Valles * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA22xxx/PIA22584_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA22584.jpg) (1390 x 2643) (249.8 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA22584.tif) (1390 x 2643) (2.5 MB)

Caption:


Context image

Today's VIS image is located near the beginning of Kasei Valles. At the top of the image is the relative high land of Lunae Planum. The Kasei Valles channel is just below. The bottom part of the image is an eroded surface that forms an island in the channel. The surface of the island has been modified by the flow of water. The linear features forming right angle intersections were caused by tectonic forces in the region.
