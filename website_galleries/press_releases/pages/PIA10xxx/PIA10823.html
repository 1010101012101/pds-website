# Cerulli Channels * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA10xxx/PIA10823_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10823.jpg) (1390 x 2922) (353.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10823.tif) (1390 x 2922) (4.1 MB)

Caption:


Context image for PIA10823
Cerulli Channels

The multiple channels at the bottom of the image are dissecting the northern rim of Cerulli Crater.

Image information: VIS instrument. Latitude 33.2°N, Longitude 22.3°E. 19 meter/pixel resolution.

Note: this THEMIS visual image has not been radiometrically nor geometrically calibrated for this preliminary release. An empirical correction has been performed to remove instrumental effects. A linear shift has been applied in the cross-track and down-track direction to approximate spacecraft and planetary motion. Fully calibrated and geometrically projected images will be released through the Planetary Data System in accordance with Project policies at a later time.
