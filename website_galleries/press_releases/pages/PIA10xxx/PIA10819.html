# Polar Dunes * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA10xxx/PIA10819_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10819.jpg) (1125 x 2900) (163.0 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10819.tif) (1125 x 2900) (3.3 MB)

Caption:


Context image for PIA10819
Polar Dunes

The dunes in this polar crater are starting to darken as the sun warms the surface and the frost sublimates (becomes gas).

Image information: VIS instrument. Latitude 79.2°N, Longitude 61.1°E. 20 meter/pixel resolution.

Note: this THEMIS visual image has not been radiometrically nor geometrically calibrated for this preliminary release. An empirical correction has been performed to remove instrumental effects. A linear shift has been applied in the cross-track and down-track direction to approximate spacecraft and planetary motion. Fully calibrated and geometrically projected images will be released through the Planetary Data System in accordance with Project policies at a later time.
