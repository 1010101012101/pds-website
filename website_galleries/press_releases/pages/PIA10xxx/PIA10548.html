# Rebel Base in Range * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA10xxx/PIA10548_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10548.jpg) (992 x 889) (44.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10548.tif) (992 x 889) (2.6 MB)

Caption:

Gray Mimas appears to hover above the colorful rings.

The large crater seen on the right side of the moon is named for William Herschel, who discovered Mimas in 1789.

Images taken using red, green and blue spectral filters were combined to create this full color view. The images were acquired with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Sept. 9, 2007 at a distance of approximately 3.151 million kilometers (1.958 million miles) from Mimas and at a Sun-Mimas-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 34 degrees. Image scale is 19 kilometers (12 miles) per pixel.

Background Info:
