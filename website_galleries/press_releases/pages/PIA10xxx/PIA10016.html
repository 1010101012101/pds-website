# "Scarecrow" * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA10xxx/PIA10016_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10016.jpg) (1413 x 928) (130.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10016.tif) (1413 x 928) (3.9 MB)

Caption:

The team developing NASA's Mars Science Laboratory calls this test rover "Scarecrow" because the vehicle does not include a computer brain. Mobility engineers use this test rover to evaluate mobility and suspension performance.

Background Info:

The Mars Science Laboratory rover is in development for launch in 2009. JPL, a division of the California Institute of Technology, Pasadena, manages the mission for the NASA Science Mission Directorate, Washington.
