# Opportunity at Work Inside Victoria Crater * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA10xxx/PIA10077_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10077.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (117.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10077.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

NASA's Mars Exploration Rover Opportunity used its front hazard-identification camera to capture this wide-angle view of its robotic arm extended to a rock in a bright-toned layer inside Victoria Crater.

The image was taken during the rover's 1,322nd Martian day, or sol (Oct. 13, 2007).

Victoria Crater has a scalloped shape of alternating alcoves and promontories around the crater's circumference. Opportunity descended into the crater two weeks earlier, within an alcove called "Duck Bay." Counterclockwise around the rim, just to the right of the arm in this image, is a promontory called "Cabo Frio."
