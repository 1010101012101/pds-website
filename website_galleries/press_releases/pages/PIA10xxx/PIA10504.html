# Mimas Above the Haze * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA10xxx/PIA10504_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA10504.jpg) (1020 x 1020) (14.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA10504.tif) (1020 x 1020) (1.0 MB)

Caption:

Mimas hangs above the hazy skies of Saturn.

This view looks toward the leading hemisphere of Mimas (396 kilometers, or 246 miles across). North is up and rotated 25 degrees to the left.

The image was taken with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Sept. 20, 2008 using a spectral filter sensitive to wavelengths of infrared light centered at 727 nanometers. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1 million kilometers (638,000 miles) from Mimas. Image scale is 6 kilometers (4 miles) per pixel.
