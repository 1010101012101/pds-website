# Coprates Chasma * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA12xxx/PIA12392_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA12392.jpg) (320 x 1808) (82.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA12392.tif) (320 x 1808) (579.4 kB)

Caption:


Context image
Credit: NASA/JPL/MOLA

Today's daytime IR image shows part of Coprates Chasma, further east than yesterday's image. Again the floor of the chasma contains various deposits, including dune fields.

Image information: IR instrument. Latitude -14.8N, Longitude 302.3°E. 122 meter/pixel resolution.

Note: this THEMIS visual image has not been radiometrically nor geometrically calibrated for this preliminary release. An empirical correction has been performed to remove instrumental effects. A linear shift has been applied in the cross-track and down-track direction to approximate spacecraft and planetary motion. Fully calibrated and geometrically projected images will be released through the Planetary Data System in accordance with Project policies at a later time.
