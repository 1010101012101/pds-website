# Chain of Secondary Craters in Mare Orientale * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA12xxx/PIA12999_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA12999.jpg) (1001 x 1001) (130.8 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA12999.tif) (1001 x 1001) (1.0 MB)

Caption:

A small secondary crater chain near the southwestern margin of Mare Orientale, within the Inner Rook Mountains. The ~125-meter-long chain lies within one of the Constellation Program regions of interest in the Orientale multi-ring basin. Image is about 530 meters across, M112224591R.

Background Info:

NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center built and manages the mission for the Exploration Systems Mission Directorate at NASA Headquarters in Washington. The Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera was designed to acquire data for landing site certification and to conduct polar illumination studies and global mapping. Operated by Arizona State University, LROC consists of a pair of narrow-angle cameras (NAC) and a single wide-angle camera (WAC). The mission is expected to return over 70 terabytes of image data.
