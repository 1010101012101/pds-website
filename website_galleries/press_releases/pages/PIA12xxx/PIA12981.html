# Dark Slope Streak * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA12xxx/PIA12981_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA12981.jpg) (1334 x 2770) (284.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA12981.tif) (1334 x 2770) (3.7 MB)

Caption:


Context image

Dark slope streaks, like the ones in this unnamed crater in Terra Sabaea, are believed to be formed when surface dust is displaced and the darker rock below is exposed. Rocks falling due to gravity likely formed these streaks.
