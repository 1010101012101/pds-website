# Luna 21 Lander * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA12xxx/PIA12976_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA12976.jpg) (450 x 450) (20.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA12976.tif) (450 x 450) (202.9 kB)

Caption:

Luna 21 lander delivered the Lunokhod 2 rover to the floor of Le Monnier crater in January 1973. The rover's tracks are visible in this image. LROC NAC Image M122007650LE.

Background Info:

NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center built and manages the mission for the Exploration Systems Mission Directorate at NASA Headquarters in Washington. The Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera was designed to acquire data for landing site certification and to conduct polar illumination studies and global mapping. Operated by Arizona State University, LROC consists of a pair of narrow-angle cameras (NAC) and a single wide-angle camera (WAC). The mission is expected to return over 70 terabytes of image data.
