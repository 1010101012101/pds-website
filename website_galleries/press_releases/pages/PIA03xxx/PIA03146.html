# Final Eros Images: Range 700 meters (2,300 feet) * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA03xxx/PIA03146_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA03146.jpg) (492 x 362) (42.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA03146.tif) (492 x 362) (209.4 kB)

Caption:

NEAR Shoemaker's image of asteroid 433 Eros taken from a range of 700 meters (2,300 feet). The image is 33 meters (108 feet) across. The large, oblong rock casting a big shadow measures 7.4 meters (24 feet) across.

Background Info:

Built and managed by The Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory, Laurel, Maryland, NEAR was the first spacecraft launched in NASA's Discovery Program of low-cost, small-scale planetary missions. See the NEAR web page at http://near.jhuapl.edu/ for more details.
