# Boulderado * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA03xxx/PIA03140_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA03140.jpg) (1235 x 1037) (146.8 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA03140.tif) (1235 x 1037) (988.0 kB)

Caption:

One of the most recognizable surface features on Eros is the boulder-filled, concave depression at the southwestern edge of the saddle-shaped Himeros. When it was first glimpsed early in NEAR Shoemaker's orbital mission the area was informally tagged "Boulderado."
