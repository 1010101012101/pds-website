# Troughs in Tharsis * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA03xxx/PIA03994_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA03994.jpg) (601 x 1291) (138.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA03994.tif) (601 x 1291) (776.9 kB)

Caption:

11 July 2005
This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image shows a suite of troughs in the Tharsis region that were also the site of some catastrophic floods. These features are located northwest of the volcano, Jovis Tholus.

Location near : 1220.7°N, 118.6°W
Image width : ~3 km (~1.9 mi)
Illumination from : lower left
Season : Northern Autumn
