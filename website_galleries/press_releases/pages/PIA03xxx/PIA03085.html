# Terby's Layers * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA03xxx/PIA03085_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA03085.jpg) (1024 x 1728) (232.0 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA03085.tif) (1024 x 1728) (1.8 MB)

Caption:

2 November 2005
This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image shows light-toned, layered, sedimentary rock exposures in Terby Crater, just north of Hellas Planitia. These rocks might have formed from sediment deposited in a lake or a larger Hellas-filling sea.
