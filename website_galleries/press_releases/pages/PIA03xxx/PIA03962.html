# Tikhonravov Layers * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA03xxx/PIA03962_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA03962.jpg) (672 x 1220) (119.8 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA03962.tif) (672 x 1220) (820.9 kB)

Caption:

23 June 2005
This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image shows layered sedimentary rocks exposed by erosion, then mantled by dust, in Tikhonravov Crater in central Arabia Terra. Dark slope streaks occur where some of the dust has slid down the layered slopes.

Location near : 13.8°N, 324.8°W
Image width : ~3 km (~1.9 mi)
Illumination from : lower left
Season : Northern Autumn
