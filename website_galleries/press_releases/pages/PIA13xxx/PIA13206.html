# Dark Slope Streaks * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA13xxx/PIA13206_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA13206.jpg) (1326 x 2770) (205.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA13206.tif) (1326 x 2770) (3.7 MB)

Caption:


Context image

Dark slope streaks, like the ones on the rim of a unnamed crater in Terra Sabaea, are thought to have been formed by material moving down slope due to gravity. As the lighter toned fine materials are removed, the darker toned underlying materials are exposed.
