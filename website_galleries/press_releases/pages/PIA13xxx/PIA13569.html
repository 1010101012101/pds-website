# Something Different * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA13xxx/PIA13569_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA13569.jpg) (1342 x 2770) (392.6 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA13569.tif) (1342 x 2770) (3.7 MB)

Caption:


Context image

In an image where the majority of the surface appears uniform with a few small hills, the region of fractured blocks sticks out as "something different." The reason that the material is different is unknown; it could be the remnants of crater ejecta, or an area of a different type of rock.
