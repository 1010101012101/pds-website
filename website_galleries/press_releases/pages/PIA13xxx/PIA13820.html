# Approaching Aitken Crater - Vertregt J * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA13xxx/PIA13820_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA13820.jpg) (1400 x 1400) (304.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA13820.tif) (1400 x 1400) (2.0 MB)

Caption:

As LRO slewed to obliquely view Aitken crater (17 January Featured Image), the northern edge of Vertregt J was serendipitously captured by the NAC. North to the left, image is about 6 kilometers (3.7 miles) wide.

Background Info:

NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center built and manages the mission for the Exploration Systems Mission Directorate at NASA Headquarters in Washington. The Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera was designed to acquire data for landing site certification and to conduct polar illumination studies and global mapping. Operated by Arizona State University, LROC consists of a pair of narrow-angle cameras (NAC) and a single wide-angle camera (WAC). The mission is expected to return over 70 terabytes of image data.
