# Ejecta Boulders * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA07xxx/PIA07504_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA07504.jpg) (2048 x 2160) (490.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA07504.tif) (2048 x 2160) (3.5 MB)

Caption:

28 March 2005
This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image shows a 1.5 meters per pixel (5 ft/pixel) view of a ~1 km wide crater. Boulders ejected by the impact that formed the crater can be seen in the ejecta blanket.

Location near : 34.1°N, 247.7°W
Image width : ~3 km (~1.9 mi)
Illumination from : lower left
Season : Northern Spring
