# Ulysses at Jupiter * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA18xxx/PIA18173_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA18173.jpg) (1191 x 849) (148.7 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA18173.tif) (1191 x 849) (3.0 MB)

Caption:

An artist's impression of the Ulysses spacecraft at Jupiter. Ulysses used Jupiter's powerful gravity to hurl it out of the Plane of the Ecliptic (where most planets and satellites orbit) so it could study the polar regions of the Sun.
