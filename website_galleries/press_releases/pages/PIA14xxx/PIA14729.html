# Mercury Transit Across the Sun * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA14xxx/PIA14729_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Movie Download Options](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/animation/PIA14729) * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA14729.jpg) (854 x 480) (52.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA14729.tif) (854 x 480) (1.2 MB)

Caption:

On May 9, 2016, Mercury passed directly between the Sun and Earth, making a transit of the Sun. Mercury transits happen about 13 times each century. NASA's SDO studies the Sun 24/7 and captured the eight-hour event. The images of Mercury's journey across the Sun were taken in a wavelength of extreme ultraviolet light.
