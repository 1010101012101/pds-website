# Small Dunes * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA14xxx/PIA14567_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA14567.jpg) (1344 x 2782) (450.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA14567.tif) (1344 x 2782) (3.7 MB)

Caption:


Context image

With the extensive amount of dust and sandy materials on Mars, dunes are a pervasive feature. Many dunes are small, like the ones in this VIS image. The bright "squiggles" in this image are small dunes. The large hill is actually part of a long, ridge system called Oenotria Scapuli.
