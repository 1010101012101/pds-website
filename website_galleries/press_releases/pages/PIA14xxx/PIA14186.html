# South Polar Layers * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA14xxx/PIA14186_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA14186.jpg) (933 x 2771) (370.0 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA14186.tif) (933 x 2771) (2.6 MB)

Caption:


Context image

Different lighting can hide or reveal different features on the surface of Mars. In today's VIS image, the lighting was perfect to reveal the details of the layering of the south polar cap.

Orbit Number: 41762 Latitude: -86.0077 Longitude: 101.902 Instrument: VIS Captured: 2011-05-14 23:17
