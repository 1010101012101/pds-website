# Dawn XMO2 Image 11 * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA21xxx/PIA21231_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA21231.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (227.0 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA21231.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

This image shows a portion of the northern hemisphere of Ceres, as seen by NASA's Dawn spacecraft. Cozobi Crater is the sharply defined impact feature at top left. At far right is Victa Crater. Abellio Crater is the sharply defined crater with a central peak just below center.

Dawn took this image on Oct. 18 from its second extended-mission science orbit (XMO2), at a distance of about 920 miles (1,480 kilometers) above the surface. The image resolution is about 460 feet (140 meters) per pixel.

Background Info:
