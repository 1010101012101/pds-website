# Dawn XMO2 Image 10 * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA21xxx/PIA21230_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA21230.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (184.6 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA21230.tif) (1024 x 1024) (873.3 kB)

Caption:

This image of the limb of dwarf planet Ceres shows a section of the northern hemisphere. A shadowy portion of Occator Crater can be seen at the lower right -- its bright "spot" areas are outside of the frame of view. Part of Kaikara Crater (45 miles, 72 kilometers in diameter) is visible at top left.

Dawn took this image on Oct. 17 from its second extended-mission science orbit (XMO2), at a distance of about 920 miles (1,480 kilometers) above the surface. The image resolution is about 460 feet (140 meters) per pixel.
