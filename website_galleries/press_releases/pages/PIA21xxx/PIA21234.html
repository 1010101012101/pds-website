# Dawn XMO2 Image 14 * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA21xxx/PIA21234_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA21234.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (248.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA21234.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

Sintana Crater is seen on the left side of this image of Ceres from NASA's Dawn spacercraft. The crater's central peak casts a shadow over its western flank.

At lower right, the rim of Darzamat peeks into view.

Dawn took this image on Oct. 19 from its second extended-mission science orbit (XMO2), at a distance of about 920 miles (1,480 kilometers) above the surface. The image resolution is about 460 feet (140 meters) per pixel.

Background Info:
