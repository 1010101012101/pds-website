# Nap Time for New Horizons: NASA Spacecraft Enters Hibernation * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA21xxx/PIA21589_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA21589.jpg) (1700 x 2004) (265.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA21589.tif) (1700 x 2004) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

NASA's New Horizons spacecraft has eased into a long summer's nap, entering a hibernation phase on April 7 that will last until early September.

View the full article

Background Info:

The Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory in Laurel, Maryland, designed, built, and operates the New Horizons spacecraft, and manages the mission for NASA's Science Mission Directorate. The Southwest Research Institute, based in San Antonio, leads the science team, payload operations and encounter science planning. New Horizons is part of the New Frontiers Program managed by NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, Alabama.
