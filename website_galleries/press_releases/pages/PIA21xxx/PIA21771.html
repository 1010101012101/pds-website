# Juno Over Jupiter's South Pole (Illustration) * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA21xxx/PIA21771_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA21771.jpg) (2550 x 3300) (508.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA21771.tif) (2550 x 3300) (15.4 MB)

Caption:

This illustration depicts NASA's Juno spacecraft soaring over Jupiter's south pole.

Background Info:

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, Pasadena, California, manages the Juno mission for the principal investigator, Scott Bolton, of Southwest Research Institute in San Antonio. The Juno mission is part of the New Frontiers Program managed at NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, Alabama. Lockheed Martin Space Systems, Denver, built the spacecraft. JPL is a division of Caltech in Pasadena.

More information about Juno is online at http://www.nasa.gov/juno and http://missionjuno.swri.edu .
