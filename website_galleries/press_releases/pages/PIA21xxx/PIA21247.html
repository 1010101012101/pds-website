# Dawn XMO2 Image 27 * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA21xxx/PIA21247_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA21247.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (227.4 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA21247.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

This view from NASA's Dawn spacecraft shows the rim of Yalode Crater on Ceres at upper left. Yalode is one of the largest impact basins on Ceres, with a diameter of 160 miles (260 kilometers). For a view of Yalode from higher altitude, along with other prominent Ceres features, see PIA20019 .

Dawn took this image on Oct. 22, 2016, from its second extended-mission science orbit (XMO2), at a distance of about 920 miles (1,480 kilometers) above the surface. The image resolution is about 460 feet (140 meters) per pixel.
