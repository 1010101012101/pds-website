# Sojourner's First Images From Mars * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA04xxx/PIA04312_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA04312.jpg) (788 x 410) (48.4 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA04312.tif) (788 x 410) (192.3 kB)

Caption:

These images are views of the Mars Pathfinder Lander's forward ramp before (top image) and after (bottom image) deployment. Some data from the before image was lost due to rover-lander communication problems.

Background Info:

Photojournal note: Sojourner spent 83 days of a planned seven-day mission exploring the Martian terrain, acquiring images, and taking chemical, atmospheric and other measurements. The final data transmission received from Pathfinder was at 10:23 UTC on September 27, 1997. Although mission managers tried to restore full communications during the following five months, the successful mission was terminated on March 10, 1998.
