# Polar Layers * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA04xxx/PIA04145_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA04145.jpg) (672 x 1248) (100.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA04145.tif) (672 x 1248) (839.7 kB)

Caption:

12 August 2005
This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image shows a slope upon which are exposed some of the layered materials that underlie the south polar cap of Mars. The layers are generally considered to be sediments--perhaps dust--that may have been cemented by water ice.
