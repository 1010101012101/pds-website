# Martian Dust Devil Tracks * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA04xxx/PIA04502_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA04502.jpg) (540 x 810) (108.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA04502.tif) (540 x 810) (438.1 kB)

Caption:

MGS MOC Release No. MOC2-351, 5 May 2003

This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image portrays a plethora of dark streaks created by passing dust devils during early summer in the martian southern hemisphere. The picture covers an area about 3 km (1.9 mi) wide near 40.2°S, 237.7°W. Sunlight illuminates the scene from the upper left.
