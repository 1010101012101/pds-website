# South Polar Cloud Animation * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA06xxx/PIA06124_med.gif) for a larger view * [Movie Download Options](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/animation/PIA06124)

Caption:

This animated gif of the south polar region of Titan was acquired over an 11.5-hour period on Oct. 23, 2004, as the Cassini spacecraft approached its first close encounter with Saturn's smoggy moon. The images were acquired with a near-infrared filter, which sees through the Titan stratospheric haze and reveals surface and lower tropospheric features. Aside from the slow rotation of the planet, a prominent polar cloud field often observed in ground-based images can be seen to evolve over the 11.5-hour period. The cloud evolution is complex and appears to include a general diverging motion.
