# Wind Erosion * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA19xxx/PIA19502_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA19502.jpg) (1368 x 2639) (451.6 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA19502.tif) (1368 x 2639) (3.6 MB)

Caption:


Context image

Long term winds have etched the surface in Memnonia Sulci. Partial cemented surface materials are easily eroded by the wind, forming linear ridges called yardangs. The multiple direction of yardangs in this VIS image indicate that there were at least two different wind directions in this area.
