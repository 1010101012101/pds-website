# Dust Storms * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA11xxx/PIA11878_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA11878.jpg) (556 x 6256) (163.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA11878.tif) (556 x 6256) (3.5 MB)

Caption:

A common feature in spring dust storms in the southern hemisphere is thin 'streams' of clouds. This feature is typically located at or near the storm front.

Image information: VIS instrument. Latitude -67.6N, Longitude 44.8°E. 34 meter/pixel resolution.

Note: this THEMIS visual image has not been radiometrically nor geometrically calibrated for this preliminary release. An empirical correction has been performed to remove instrumental effects. A linear shift has been applied in the cross-track and down-track direction to approximate spacecraft and planetary motion. Fully calibrated and geometrically projected images will be released through the Planetary Data System in accordance with Project policies at a later time.
