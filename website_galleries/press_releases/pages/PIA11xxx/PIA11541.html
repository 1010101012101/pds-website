# Shadow in the Cassini Division * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA11xxx/PIA11541_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA11541.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (197.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA11541.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

The shadow of the moon Mimas is seen through the unlit side of the Cassini Division of Saturn's rings.

As Saturn approaches its August 2009 equinox, the planet's moons cast shadows onto the rings. To learn more about this special time and to see a movie of a moon's shadow moving across the rings, see PIA11651 .

To watch a movie of Mimas' shadow moving across the unlit side of the rings, see PIA11660 .

This view looks toward the unilluminated side of the rings from about 53 degrees above the ringplane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on June 1, 2009. The view was obtained at a distance of approximately 1.7 million kilometers (1.1 million miles) from Saturn and at a Sun-Saturn-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 94 degrees. Image scale is 10 kilometers (6 miles) per pixel.
