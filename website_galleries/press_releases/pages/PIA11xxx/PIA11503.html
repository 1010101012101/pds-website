# Petite Moon * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA11xxx/PIA11503_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA11503.jpg) (1018 x 1450) (45.7 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA11503.tif) (1018 x 1450) (1.5 MB)

Caption:

A bright arc within Saturn's faint G ring holds a tiny gift.

A small moonlet is just visible as a short streak near the ansa of the G ring arc in the top of two versions of the same image. The second (bottom) version of the image has been brightened to enhance the visibility of the G ring. The other streaks in this version of the image are stars smeared by the camera's long exposure time of 26 seconds. This version of the image shows a gap in the G ring which was faintly visible in an earlier Cassini movie (see PIA08327 ).

The moonlet, dubbed S/2008 S 1, is likely a major source of the material of the G ring (see PIA11148 ).

This view looks toward the sunlit side of the rings from about 1 degrees below the ring plane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft narrow-angle camera on Jan. 28, 2009. The view was acquired at a distance of approximately 1.2 million kilometers (746,000 miles) from Saturn and at a Sun-Saturn-spacecraft, or phase, angle of 27 degrees. Image scale is 7 kilometers (4 miles) per pixel.
