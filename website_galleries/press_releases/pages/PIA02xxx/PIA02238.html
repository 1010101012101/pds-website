# Titan's Thick Haze Layer * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA02xxx/PIA02238_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA02238.jpg) (485 x 580) (14.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA02238.tif) (485 x 580) (408.2 kB)

Caption:

Titan's thick haze layer is shown in this enhanced Voyager 1 image taken Nov. 12, 1980 at a distance of 435,000 kilometers (270,000 miles). Voyager images of Saturn's largest moon show Titan completely enveloped by haze that merges with a darker "hood" or cloud layer over the north pole. Such a mantle is not present at the south pole. At Voyager's closest approach to Titan on Nov. 11, 1980, spacecraft instruments found that the moon has a substantial atmosphere, far denser than that of Mars and possibly denser than Earth's.
