# Neptune Clouds on the Dark Spot * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA02xxx/PIA02222_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA02222.jpg) (400 x 400) (11.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA02222.tif) (400 x 400) (28.6 kB)

Caption:

This photo was taken by Voyager 2's wide-angle camera. Light at methane wavelengths is mostly absorbed in the deeper atmosphere. The bright, white feature is a high-altitude cloud just south of the Great Dark Spot. Other, smaller clouds associated with the Great Dark Spot are white, and are also at high altitudes.
