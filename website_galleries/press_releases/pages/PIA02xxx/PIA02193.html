# Devil-Streaked Plain * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA02xxx/PIA02193_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA02193.jpg) (672 x 1008) (135.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA02193.tif) (672 x 1008) (678.3 kB)

Caption:

19 February 2006
This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image shows dark streaks on a plain south of the giant impact basin, Hellas Planitia. The streaks map the routes traveled by dozens of individual southern spring and early summer dust devils.

Location near : 68.4°S, 296.1°W
Image width : ~3 km (~1.9 mi)
Illumination from : upper left
Season : Southern Summer
