# Lineated Terrain * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA02xxx/PIA02428_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA02428.jpg) (356 x 311) (24.4 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA02428.tif) (356 x 311) (101.8 kB)

Caption:

Lineated terrain not clearly related to any crater or basin. Widest valleys are 10 km across. Area centered at 8 degrees S, 148 degrees W. This image (FDS 246) was taken during the spacecraft's first encounter with Mercury.

Background Info:

The Mariner 10 mission, managed by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory for NASA's Office of Space Science, explored Venus in February 1974 on the way to three encounters with Mercury-in March and September 1974 and in March 1975. The spacecraft took more than 7,000 photos of Mercury, Venus, the Earth and the Moon.
