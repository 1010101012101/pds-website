# Io - High Res Limb * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA02xxx/PIA02232_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA02232.jpg) (794 x 794) (37.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA02232.tif) (794 x 794) (292.4 kB)

Caption:

This picture of Io was taken on the morning of March 5 at a rang eof 75,445 kilometers. The area shown is at latitude 15 south longitude 244 . Many depressions and elevations are shown. The light is coming from the left, so a depression has a bright right wall and shadow on the left wall. The depressions are complex in shape and do not resemble impact craters. Two of the depressions are joined by a shallow trough. The elevations are irregular and comical. They are cut by linear and irregular troughs. The surface is smooth and plain-like, that is, the surface is not pockmarked by abundant impact craters so are probably geologically young.
