# Saturn's F Ring * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA02xxx/PIA02283_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA02283.jpg) (354 x 384) (6.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA02283.tif) (354 x 384) (22.0 kB)

Caption:

Saturn's F, or outermost ring was photographed from the un-illuminated face of the rings by Voyager 1 at a range of 750,000 kilometers (470,000 miles). Complex structure is evident, with several components seen. Two narrow, braided, bright rings that trace distinct orbits are evident. Visible is a broader, very diffuse component about 35 kilometers (20 miles) in width. Also seen are "knots," which probably are local clumps of ring material, but may be mini-moons.
