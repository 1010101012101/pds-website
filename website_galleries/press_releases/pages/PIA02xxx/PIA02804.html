# Major Martian Volcanoes from MOLA - Arsia Mons * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA02xxx/PIA02804_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA02804.jpg) (524 x 462) (52.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA02804.tif) (524 x 462) (231.1 kB)

Caption:

Two views of Arsia Mons, the southern most of the Tharsis montes, shown as topography draped over a Viking image mosaic. MOLA topography clearly shows the caldera structure and the flank massive breakout that produced a major side lobe. The vertical exaggeration is 10:1.
