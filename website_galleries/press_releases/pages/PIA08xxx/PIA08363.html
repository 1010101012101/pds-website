# Giant Lake on Titan * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA08xxx/PIA08363_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08363.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (42.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08363.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:


Annotated Version

This view of Titan taken on Feb. 25, 2007, reveals a giant lake-like feature in Titan's North Polar Region. It is approximately 1,100 kilometers (680 miles) long and has a surface area slightly smaller than that of Earth's largest lake, the Caspian Sea.
