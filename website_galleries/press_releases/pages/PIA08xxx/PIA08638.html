# Northern Gullies * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA08xxx/PIA08638_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08638.jpg) (1672 x 2520) (716.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08638.tif) (1672 x 2520) (4.2 MB)

Caption:

3 August 2006
This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image shows a group of gullies formed on the equator-facing wall of a north mid-latitude crater. Gullies such as these might have formed from the erosive forces of liquid water.

Location near : 49.4°N, 56.1°W
Image width : ~3 km (~1.9 mi)
Illumination from : lower left
Season : Northern Spring
