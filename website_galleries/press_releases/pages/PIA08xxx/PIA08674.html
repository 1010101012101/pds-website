# High Plains Ring * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA08xxx/PIA08674_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08674.jpg) (672 x 1174) (272.4 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08674.tif) (672 x 1174) (789.9 kB)

Caption:

18 August 2006
This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image shows a ring marking the location of a nearly-filled, nearly-buried impact crater on the martian northern plains. Remnants of bright, seasonal frost occur in some polygonal cracks on the plain.

Location near : 62.9°N, 96.0°W
Image width : ~3 km (~1.9 mi)
Illumination from : lower left
Season : Northern Spring
