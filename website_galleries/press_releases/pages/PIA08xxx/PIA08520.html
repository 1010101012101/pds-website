# Layer-Upon-Layer * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA08xxx/PIA08520_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA08520.jpg) (1024 x 2696) (307.6 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA08520.tif) (1024 x 2696) (2.8 MB)

Caption:

7 June 2006
This Mars Global Surveyor (MGS) Mars Orbiter Camera (MOC) image shows repeated layers of material, possibly a combination of dust and ice, or perhaps ancient sedimentary rock, exposed by erosion on a slope in the south polar region of Mars.

Location near : 83.9°S, 257.8°W
Image width : ~3 km (~1.9 mi)
Illumination from : upper left
Season : Southern Summer
