# Location of DAN on Curiosity * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA16xxx/PIA16082_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA16082.jpg) (1395 x 1195) (229.2 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA16082.tif) (1395 x 1195) (5.0 MB)

Caption:

This image of NASA's Curiosity rover shows the location of the two components of the Dynamic Albedo of Neutrons instrument. The neutron generator is mounted on the right hip (visible in this view), and the detectors are on the opposite hip.

Background Info:

JPL manages the Mars Science Laboratory/Curiosity for NASA's Science Mission Directorate in Washington. The rover was designed, developed and assembled at JPL, a division of the California Institute of Technology in Pasadena.

For more about NASA's Curiosity mission, visit: http://www.jpl.nasa.gov/msl , http://www.nasa.gov/mars , and http://marsprogram.jpl.nasa.gov/msl .
