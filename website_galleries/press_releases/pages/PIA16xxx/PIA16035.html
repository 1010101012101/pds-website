# A Set of Blast Marks in Color, Right Side * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA16xxx/PIA16035_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA16035.jpg) (1920 x 1080) (139.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA16035.tif) (1920 x 1080) (6.2 MB)

Caption:

This cut-out from a color panorama image taken by NASA's Curiosity rover shows the effects of the descent stage's rocket engines blasting the ground. It comes from the right side of the thumbnail panorama obtained by Curiosity's Mast Camera. (See PIA16029 .)

Background Info:

JPL manages the Mars Science Laboratory/Curiosity for NASA's Science Mission Directorate in Washington. The rover was designed, developed and assembled at JPL, a division of the California Institute of Technology in Pasadena.

For more about NASA's Curiosity mission, visit: http://www.jpl.nasa.gov/msl , http://www.nasa.gov/mars , and http://marsprogram.jpl.nasa.gov/msl .
