# Dawn LAMO Image 112 * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA20xxx/PIA20652_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA20652.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (157.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA20652.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

This image of Ceres shows a crater near the dwarf planet's equator. The view is centered at approximately 6 degrees south latitude, 230 degrees east longitude.

Dawn took this image on March 25, 2016, from its low-altitude mapping orbit, at a distance of about 240 miles (385 kilometers) above the surface. The image resolution is 120 feet (35 meters) per pixel.
