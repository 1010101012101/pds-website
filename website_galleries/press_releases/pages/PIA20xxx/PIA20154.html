# Craters of All Ages and Sizes * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA20xxx/PIA20154_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA20154.jpg) (903 x 471) (101.6 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA20154.tif) (903 x 471) (780.5 kB)

Caption:

Locations of more than 1,000 craters mapped on Pluto by NASA's New Horizons mission indicate a wide range of surface ages, which likely means that Pluto has been geologically active throughout its history.

Background Info:

The Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory in Laurel, Maryland, designed, built, and operates the New Horizons spacecraft, and manages the mission for NASA's Science Mission Directorate. The Southwest Research Institute, based in San Antonio, leads the science team, payload operations and encounter science planning. New Horizons is part of the New Frontiers Program managed by NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, Alabama.
