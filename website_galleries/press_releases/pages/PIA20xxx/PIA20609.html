# Dark Slope Streaks * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA20xxx/PIA20609_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA20609.jpg) (1384 x 2641) (194.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA20609.tif) (1384 x 2641) (2.1 MB)

Caption:


Context image

Today's VIS image shows part of the rim of an unnamed crater in Terra Sabaea. Numerous dark streaks are visible. One theory of how the streaks form suggests that a rock or block of material rolls down the slope, removing or disturbing the surface dust and exposing the darker rock underneath.
