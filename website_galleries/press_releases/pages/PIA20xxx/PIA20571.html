# Dawn LAMO Image 76 * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA20xxx/PIA20571_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA20571.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (194.3 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA20571.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.0 MB)

Caption:

Sekhet Crater on Ceres has prominent shadows accentuating its central peak and mounds of material that have slumped downward from its walls. The name Sekhet comes from the name of the Egyptian goddess of cultivated lands and fields.

The crater is 25 miles (41 kilometers) wide. A different view of Sekhet is available in PIA20390 .

NASA's Dawn spacecraft took this image on January 29, 2016, in its low-altitude mapping orbit, at a distance of about 240 miles (385 kilometers) above the surface. The image resolution is 120 feet (35 meters) per pixel.
