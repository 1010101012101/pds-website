# Dawn LAMO Image 107 * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA20xxx/PIA20647_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA20647.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (168.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA20647.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

This pair of craters is located in the northern hemisphere of Ceres. The wall of the older crater (lower of the two) has partially collapsed where it adjoins the rim of its younger neighbor.

NASA's Dawn spacecraft spotted these features on January 25, 2016, in its low-altitude mapping orbit, at a distance of about 240 miles (385 kilometers) above the surface. The image resolution is 120 feet (35 meters) per pixel.
