# Dawn LAMO Image 71 * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA20xxx/PIA20566_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA20566.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (193.8 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA20566.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

Achita Crater on Ceres was named for the Nigerian god of agriculture. NASA's Dawn spacecraft took this image of the crater on January 15, 2016. Achita measures about 25 miles (40 kilometers) in diameter and is located in the northern hemisphere.

Dawn spotted Achita from its low-altitude mapping orbit, at a distance of about 240 miles (385 kilometers) above the surface. The image resolution is 120 feet (35 meters) per pixel.
