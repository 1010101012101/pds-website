# Dawn LAMO Image 143 * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA20xxx/PIA20863_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA20863.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (223.0 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA20863.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

This view from NASA's Dawn spacecraft features Liber Crater (14 miles, 23 kilometers wide) in Ceres' northern hemisphere, at right.

Dawn took this image on June 17, 2016, from its low-altitude mapping orbit, at a distance of about 240 miles (385 kilometers) above the surface. The image resolution is 120 feet (35 meters) per pixel.

Background Info:

Dawn's mission is managed by JPL for NASA's Science Mission Directorate in Washington. Dawn is a project of the directorate's Discovery Program, managed by NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center in Huntsville, Alabama. UCLA is responsible for overall Dawn mission science.
