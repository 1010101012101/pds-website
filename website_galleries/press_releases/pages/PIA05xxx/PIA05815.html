# Spirit's View on Sol 110 in 3-D * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA05xxx/PIA05815_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA05815.jpg) (7704 x 1135) (944.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA05815.tif) (7704 x 1135) (17.5 MB)

Caption:

This three-dimensional, cylindrical-perspective projection was assembled from images taken by the navigation camera on the Mars Exploration Rover Spirit on sol 110 (April 24, 2004) at a region dubbed "site 35." Spirit is sitting approximately 33 meters (100 feet) away from the northeast rim of "Missoula" crater.

See PIA05816 for left eye view and PIA05817 for right eye view of this 3-D cylindrical-perspective projection.
