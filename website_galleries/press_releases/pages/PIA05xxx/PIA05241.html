# Inner Adirondack * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA05xxx/PIA05241_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA05241.jpg) (640 x 480) (27.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA05241.tif) (640 x 480) (198.1 kB)

Caption:

This close-up image taken by the microscopic imager onboard the Mars Exploration Rover Spirit shows the rock dubbed Adirondack after a portion of its surface was ground off by the rover's rock abrasion tool. The observed area is 3 centimeters (1.2 inches) across.
