# Coordinate Map of Rocks at Pathfinder Landing Site * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA01xxx/PIA01153_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA01153.jpg) (4201 x 701) (837.1 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA01153.tif) (4201 x 701) (9.5 MB)

Caption:

Mars-local-level (LL frame) coordinate map of rocks counted at the Mars Pathfinder landing site. Positions, apparent diameters (D), and heights (H) were measured to the nearest centimeter in the Mars map virtual reality environment constructed from the "Monster Pan."

Background Info:

Photojournal note: Sojourner spent 83 days of a planned seven-day mission exploring the Martian terrain, acquiring images, and taking chemical, atmospheric and other measurements. The final data transmission received from Pathfinder was at 10:23 UTC on September 27, 1997. Although mission managers tried to restore full communications during the following five months, the successful mission was terminated on March 10, 1998.
