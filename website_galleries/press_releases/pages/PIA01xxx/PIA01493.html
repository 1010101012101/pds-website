# Neptune's Rings * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA01xxx/PIA01493_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA01493.jpg) (1469 x 1160) (144.6 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA01493.tif) (1469 x 1160) (369.4 kB)

Caption:

In Neptune's outermost ring, 39,000 miles out, material mysteriously clumps into three arcs. Voyager 2 acquired this image as it encountered Neptune in August of 1989.

Background Info:

JPL manages and controls the Voyager project for NASA's Office of Space Science.
