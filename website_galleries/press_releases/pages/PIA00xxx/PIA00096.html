# Three-dimensional perspective views of Venusian Terrains composed of reduced resolution left-looking synthetic-aperture radar images merged with altimetry data from the Magellan spacecraft. * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA00xxx/PIA00096_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA00096.jpg) (723 x 425) (64.0 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA00096.tif) (723 x 425) (225.6 kB)

Caption:

The view shows most of Galindo (V-40) quadrangle looking east; Atete Corona, in the foreground, is a 600-km-long and about 450-km-wide, circular volcano-tectonic feature. Coronae are believed to form over hot upwellings of magma within the Venusian mantle.
