# Three-dimensional perspective views of Venusian Terrains composed of reduced resolution left-looking synthetic-aperture radar images merged with altimetry data from the Magellan spacecraft. * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA00xxx/PIA00095_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA00095.jpg) (647 x 425) (68.5 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA00095.tif) (647 x 425) (268.7 kB)

Caption:

The view shows part of Galindo (V40) quadrangle looking north; 200 km wide Nagavonyi Corona is in the foreground. Coronae are roughly circular, volcanic features believed to form over hot upwellings of magma within the Venusian mantle. A 2-km shield volcano behind the corona partly buries the Phoebo Regio highlands (shown as radar-bright mounds).
