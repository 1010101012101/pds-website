# Curiosity's Hazard Cameras Ready for Action * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA15xxx/PIA15960_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Movie Download Options](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/animation/PIA15960) * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA15960.jpg) (1279 x 720) (89.4 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA15960.tif) (1279 x 720) (2.8 MB)

Caption:


Click on the image for the animation

This artist's animation shows the location of a set of Hazard-Avoidance cameras on the back of NASA's Curiosity rover. Each camera is protected by a clear dust cover, shown here popping off.

Background Info:

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, a division of the California Institute of Technology, Pasadena, manages the Mars Science Laboratory mission for the NASA Science Mission Directorate, Washington.
