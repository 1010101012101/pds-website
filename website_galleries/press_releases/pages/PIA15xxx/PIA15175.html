# Dawn Orbiting Over Vesta * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA15xxx/PIA15175_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA15175.jpg) (1024 x 1024) (145.0 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA15175.tif) (1024 x 1024) (1.1 MB)

Caption:

This image of the giant asteroid Vesta was obtained by NASA's Dawn spacecraft in the evening Nov. 27 PST (early morning Nov. 28, UTC), as it was spiraling down from its high altitude mapping orbit to low altitude mapping orbit. Low altitude mapping orbit is the closest orbit Dawn will be making, at an average of 130 miles (210 kilometers) above the giant asteroid's surface. The framing camera obtained this image of an area in the northern mid-latitudes of Vesta from an altitude of about 140 miles (230 kilometers).

Background Info:

The Dawn mission to the asteroids Vesta and Ceres is managed by NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, a division of the California Institute of Technology in Pasadena, for NASA's Science Mission Directorate, Washington.
