# 3-D Anaglyph Fish-eye View of NASA's Curiosity Rover and its Rocket-Powered Descent Vehicle * Click the [image above](/press_releases/medium/PIA15xxx/PIA15180_med.jpg) for a larger view * [Full-Res JPEG](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpeg/PIA15180.jpg) (4308 x 2828) (933.9 kB) * [Full-Res TIFF](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/tiff/PIA15180.tif) (4308 x 2828) (36.5 MB)

Caption:

NASA's Curiosity rover and its rocket-powered descent vehicle pose for a portrait at JPL's Spacecraft Assembly Facility. During a nearly two-year prime mission after landing on Mars, the rover will investigate whether Gale Crater ever offered conditions favorable for microbial life, including the chemical ingredients for life.

Background Info:

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, a division of the California Institute of Technology, Pasadena, Calif., manages the Mars Science Laboratory Project for the NASA Science Mission Directorate, Washington.

More information about Curiosity is online at http://www.nasa.gov/msl or http://mars.jpl.nasa.gov/msl .
