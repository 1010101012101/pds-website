Moons visible in the image are: Mimas (398 kilometers or 247 miles across) above the rings at left; Epimetheus (116 kilometers or 72 miles across) just above the A ring; Enceladus (499 kilometers or 310 miles across) near upper right. The image was taken with the Cassini narrow angle camera on May 10, 2004, at a distance of 27.1 million kilometers (16.8 million miles) from Saturn. Image scale is 162 kilometers (101 miles) per pixel. Contrast in the image was enhanced to aid visibility.
