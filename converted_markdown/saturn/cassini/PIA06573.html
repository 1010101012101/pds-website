# PIA06573: The Giant's Shadow | [ ![The Giant's Shadow](./browse/PIA06573.jpg) ](./jpegMod/PIA06573_modest.jpg) | | Target Name: | **[Saturn](http://solarsystem.nasa.gov/planets/profile.cfm?Object=Saturn)** ---|--- Mission: | ** [Cassini](http://saturn.jpl.nasa.gov) ** Spacecraft: | ** [Cassini Orbiter](http://saturn.jpl.nasa.gov) ** Instrument: | ** Imaging Science Subsystem - Wide Angle ** Product Size: | **1024 samples x 912 lines** Produced By: | ** [CICLOPS/Space Science Institute](http://ciclops.org) ** Full-Res TIFF: | ** [PIA06573.tif](./tiff/PIA06573.tif) (285.9 kB) ** Full-Res JPEG: | ** [PIA06573.jpg](./jpeg/PIA06573.jpg) (29.52 kB) ** Medium-Res JPEG: | ** [PIA06573_modest.jpg](./jpegMod/PIA06573_modest.jpg) (29.52 kB)** **Original Caption Released with Image: ** Now, in southern summer, Saturn's shadow stretches across the sunlit southern surface of its rings. Saturn's moon Janus orbits just outside of the main rings and appears below them in this scene. Janus is absolutely dwarfed by the bulk of its gigantic parent. Janus is 181 kilometers (113 miles) across. Bands of ring material within the Cassini Division are visible here, near the outer edge of the bright B ring. The planet's night side is visible at the right. This view is from Cassini's vantage point beneath the ring plane. The image was taken in visible light with the Cassini spacecraft wide angle camera on Jan. 17, 2005, at a distance of approximately 1.2 million kilometers (746,000 miles) from Saturn. The image scale is 66 kilometers (41 miles) per pixel. Janus was brightened by a factor of two, and contrast in the scene was enhanced to aid visibility.
